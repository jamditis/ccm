# Grant Proposal Outline Generator

A tool to structure grant proposals with sections aligned to common journalism funder requirements. Helps newsrooms organize their thinking and ensure they address all required elements.

## Features

### Core Functionality
- **Funder Templates**: Pre-built structures for major journalism funders
- **Proposal Modes**: Letter of Inquiry (LOI) vs. Full Proposal
- **Section Builder**: Standard grant sections with writing guidance
- **Progress Tracking**: Visual completion tracking across sections
- **Attachment Checklist**: Track required documents

### Funder Templates
- **Generic Journalism Grant**: Standard proposal structure
- **Knight Foundation**: Innovation and community focus
- **Google News Initiative**: Business sustainability focus
- **Report for America**: Corps member applications
- **Local Media Association**: Local journalism funders

### Proposal Sections

**Full Proposal includes:**
1. Executive Summary
2. Organization Background
3. Problem Statement / Need
4. Project Description
5. Goals & Objectives
6. Methods / Activities
7. Timeline / Milestones
8. Evaluation Plan
9. Budget & Justification
10. Sustainability Plan
11. Key Personnel

**Letter of Inquiry (shorter):**
1. Executive Summary
2. Organization Background
3. Problem Statement
4. Project Description
5. Budget Summary

### Guidance Features
- Section-by-section writing prompts
- Word count suggestions
- Funder-specific tips
- Common pitfalls to avoid

### Export
- PDF outline with all sections
- Copy individual sections
- Attachment checklist

## Quick Start

1. Open `index.html` in any modern web browser
2. Select your funder template
3. Choose LOI or Full Proposal
4. Fill in project details (title, organization, amount, duration)
5. Work through each section using the guidance
6. Mark sections complete as you finish
7. Track required attachments
8. Export to PDF

## Writing Guidance

Each section includes:
- **Explanation**: What the section should contain
- **Prompts**: Specific questions to address
- **Word count**: Suggested length
- **Tips**: Funder-specific advice

### Example: Executive Summary

**Guidance**: Provide a compelling overview of your project in 2-3 paragraphs. Include: what you will do, why it matters, who will benefit, how much you need, and what success looks like.

**Prompts**:
- What problem does your project address?
- What is your proposed solution?
- What outcomes do you expect?
- How much funding do you need?

## Attachment Tracking

Common required attachments:
- 501(c)(3) Determination Letter
- Detailed Line-Item Budget
- Staff Bios / Resumes
- Board of Directors List
- Financial Statements
- Coverage Samples

Check off attachments as you prepare them to ensure you have everything ready for submission.

## Tips for Success

1. **Start with the problem**: Clearly articulate the need before describing your solution
2. **Be specific**: Use concrete numbers and examples rather than generalizations
3. **Show track record**: Demonstrate your organization's capability
4. **Plan for sustainability**: Explain how impact will continue after funding
5. **Match funder priorities**: Align your proposal with the funder's stated goals
6. **Use SMART goals**: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
