## Organization Overview

### The Campbell's Foundation

- **Established:** 1953
- **Parent Organization:** The Campbell's Company (formerly Campbell Soup Company)
- **Headquarters:** 1 Campbell Place, Camden, NJ 08103
- **Total Fiscal 2025 Grantmaking:** More than $2.6 million
- **Recent Grant Round (April 2025):** $920,000 to 46 nonprofit organizations

### Mission & Focus

The Campbell's Foundation supports communities throughout North America where Campbell's employees live and work through direct grants, employee matching gifts, and strategic partnerships with nonprofit organizations.

**Three Core Focus Areas:**
1. Increasing food access
2. Encouraging healthy living
3. Nurturing neighborhoods

---

## Grant Programs & Funding Areas

### 1. Community Impact Grants (Annual)

- **Amount:** Typically $20,000 per recipient (grants range from $1,000-$25,000)
- **Process:** Employee-nominated; organizations must be nominated by local Campbell's employees
- **Eligibility:** Organizations within 25 miles of a Campbell's plant or office
- **New Jersey Restriction:** **Limited to organizations serving Camden city specifically**
- **Application Cycle:** Fall (employee nominations)

### 2. Multi-Year Initiatives

- **Focus:** Systems change related to food access
- **Examples:**
  - Full Futures school nutrition program (5-year, $5 million commitment)
  - Food Bank of South Jersey partnership ($1.5 million gift for Center for Health, Wellness, and Nutrition)
  - Camden Food Fund (supporting food entrepreneurs in Camden)
  - Camden Food Security Collective

### 3. Employee Giving Program ("Campbell's Cares")

- **Matching Amount:** Up to $1,500 annually per employee
- **Eligible Recipients:** Any 501(c)(3) organization
- **Process:** 100% match of employee donations and volunteer time
- **Total Impact (2023):** $1.5 million donated to support more than 750 organizations

---

## What The Campbell's Foundation DOES NOT Fund

**Critical for ECM Event Planning:**

- ❌ **Event sponsorships** (explicitly stated)
- ❌ Individuals (including scholarships, loans, and sponsorships)
- ❌ Schools or government entities
- ❌ Organizations that discriminate based on race, color, ancestry, sex, gender, religion, national origin, age, disability, medical condition, genetic information, or sexual orientation
- ❌ Religious organizations whose principal purpose is propagating a particular religious faith (exception: non-sectarian programs open to general public)
- ❌ Political organizations or those primarily focused on influencing legislation

## Alternative Engagement Opportunities for ECM Event

### 1. Employee Matching Gifts Program ⭐ HIGH POTENTIAL

**How it works:**
- Individual Campbell's employees can donate to the ECM event/organization
- Campbell's Foundation matches up to $1,500 per employee annually
- 100% match of donations and volunteer time
- Foundation covers all processing fees

**Strategy for ECM:**
- Identify Campbell's employees interested in ethnic/community media
- Personal outreach to employees about ECM mission
- Could multiply donations through matching program
- Employees can volunteer at the event and receive matching for volunteer hours

### 2. In-Kind Product Donations ⭐ HIGH POTENTIAL

**Program Details:**
- Campbell's Company has an in-kind donation program
- Could provide food products for the luncheon event
- Historical precedent: Tree of Giving initiative with hundreds of soup cans donated to food banks
- Disaster relief support included product donations

**Strategy for ECM:**
- Request product donations for the February 26 luncheon
- Position as supporting diverse communities and media voices
- Could reduce event catering costs significantly
- Application through in-kind donation program

### 3. Employee Volunteer Engagement ⭐ MEDIUM POTENTIAL

**Opportunities:**

- Recruit Campbell's employees as volunteers for event setup, registration, etc.

### 4. Connection to Diversity Initiatives ⭐ MEDIUM POTENTIAL

**Strategy for ECM:**

### 5. Future Grant Consideration ⭐ LOW-MEDIUM POTENTIAL

**Requirements:**

- Employee nomination required
- Align with at least one of three focus areas

**Highest Potential:**
1. In-kind product donations for the luncheon event
2. Employee matching gifts program (individual employee donations matched up to $1,500)
3. Employee volunteer support for the event

**Medium Potential:**
4. Connection to diversity initiatives and supplier network
5. Future grant consideration through employee nomination (fall 2026)

**Recommended Strategy:**
- Focus on in-kind donation request and employee engagement for February 2026 event
- Build relationship for potential future grant support
- Position ECM as supporting Campbell's diversity and community priorities
- Emphasize local Camden/South Jersey connection

The Campbell's Foundation represents a strong potential partner for the ECM event, not through traditional sponsorship, but through creative alternative engagement that aligns with their community investment philosophy and diversity commitments.
