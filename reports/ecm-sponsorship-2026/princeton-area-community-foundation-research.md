## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Fit Assessment: LOW**

The Princeton Area Community Foundation has a **strictly defined geographic footprint** focused on central New Jersey (primarily Mercer County and immediately adjacent areas). Their discretionary grant programs - including Community Impact Grants and The Bunbury Fund - are limited to organizations serving this specific region. Unless the ECM event is located within their footprint and serves their geographic communities, **pursuit is NOT recommended**.

**Possible Exception:** Donor-advised funds at PACF have the flexibility to support organizations nationwide, but this would require identifying and cultivating a relationship with a specific fundholder interested in ethnic and community media.

## GEOGRAPHIC SERVICE AREA (CRITICAL LIMITATION)

### Exact Footprint for Discretionary Grantmaking

PACF's competitive grant programs serve only the following areas:

1. **Mercer County** (primary focus - all municipalities)
2. **Eastern Bucks County, PA** - including New Hope, Morrisville, Washington Crossing, and Yardley
3. **Northern Burlington County** - including Bordentown and Bordentown Township
4. **Southern Hunterdon County** - including Lambertville, West Amwell Township, and adjacent parts of East Amwell Township
5. **Southern Middlesex County** - including Plainsboro and Cranbury
6. **Southern Somerset County** - including Montgomery, Rocky Hill, and adjacent sections of Franklin Township (like Griggstown)

### Eligibility Requirements for Discretionary Grants

Organizations must:
- Operate and provide services **within** the geographic footprint
- Have 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status
- Be publicly supported under Section 509
- Be registered with NJ Division of Consumer Affairs as a charity in good standing

**Note:** PACF explicitly states that "grantseekers from outside of this geographic scope will not be successful in securing a grant" from their competitive programs.

## DONOR-ADVISED FUNDS (POTENTIAL PATHWAY)

### Key Finding: Geographic Flexibility

Unlike discretionary grant programs, **donor-advised funds (DAFs) at PACF can support organizations nationwide**. The foundation's materials state fundholders can "recommend grants to nonprofits across the country and beyond."

### How DAFs Work at PACF

- Fundholders make grant recommendations to charitable organizations of their choice
- PACF staff provide due diligence and knowledge of charitable landscape
- Restrictions: Cannot satisfy personal pledges, support private family foundations, or buy fundraising event tickets

### Strategy for ECM Event

If pursuing PACF despite geographic limitations, the pathway would be:
1. Identify specific fundholders at PACF who have interest in:
   - Ethnic and community media
   - Immigrant communities
   - Journalism/civic engagement
   - Statewide or regional NJ initiatives
2. Cultivate direct relationships with those fundholders
3. Request sponsorship through their donor-advised fund

**Challenge:** This requires significant research to identify appropriate fundholders and relationship building. PACF typically does not publicly disclose fundholder information.

## RECOMMENDATIONS

### Overall Assessment: DO NOT PURSUE (with exceptions noted)

**Primary Recommendation:** Do not pursue Princeton Area Community Foundation for ECM event sponsorship unless one of the following applies:

1. **Event is located within and primarily serves PACF footprint** (Mercer County and immediate surrounding areas as defined above)

2. **You have existing relationship with a PACF fundholder** who has expressed interest in ethnic/community media or statewide journalism initiatives

3. **You can partner with a Mercer County-based organization** where the collaboration genuinely serves PACF footprint communities and meets all eligibility requirements

### If Pursuing Despite Geographic Limitation

If you choose to explore PACF despite the footprint concern, take these steps:

1. **Clarify Event Location & Beneficiaries**
   - Confirm exact location of Feb 26 ECM event
   - Map which communities will be served/benefit
   - Determine if any overlap with PACF footprint

2. **Contact PACF Directly**
   - Call Danny Freund (Director of Discretionary Grantmaking) or Nelida Valentin (VP of Grants & Programs)
   - Ask specifically about:
     - Any exceptions to geographic requirements
     - Donor-advised fund opportunities for statewide initiatives
     - Potential partner organizations in their footprint
   - Phone: 609-219-1800

3. **Explore DAF Pathway**
   - Ask PACF if they can connect you with fundholders interested in journalism/media/civic engagement
   - Request information about their fundholder engagement process

### Timeline Concern

With the event on February 26, 2026 (6 weeks away), the timeline for securing PACF sponsorship through any pathway is **extremely tight**. Typical grant cycles require:
- Application preparation: 2-4 weeks
- Review process: 4-8 weeks minimum
- Board approval: Quarterly or specific meeting dates

**For this timeline, only a donor-advised fund recommendation could potentially work**, and that requires an existing relationship.

### Better Alternatives to Pursue

Given the geographic limitation and timeline, recommend prioritizing:

1. **Statewide New Jersey foundations** without geographic restrictions
2. **National foundations** supporting ethnic/community media (Knight Foundation, Democracy Fund, etc.)
3. **Corporate sponsors** with New Jersey presence and DEI/community engagement priorities
4. **Media industry funders** (News Match, American Journalism Project, etc.)
5. **Individual donors** passionate about ethnic media and journalism

## CONCLUSION

Princeton Area Community Foundation is a well-resourced ($260M+ assets), sophisticated funder with strong thematic alignment in areas of civic engagement, immigrant communities, and community building. However, their **strict geographic footprint** centered on Mercer County and immediately adjacent areas creates a fundamental barrier for an ECM event located outside this region.

The user's assessment that "we are not in their footprint" appears accurate. Unless the event location and beneficiary communities fall within PACF's defined service area, or unless you can identify a specific donor-advised fundholder willing to support this initiative, **pursuit is not recommended**.

**Effort should be redirected to foundations and sponsors with statewide or national scope that align with ethnic and community media priorities.**
