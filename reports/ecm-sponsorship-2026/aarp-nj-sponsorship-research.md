## Talking Points: ECM Luncheon Value Proposition for AARP

### Why Ethnic Media Matters to AARP's Mission

**1. Trusted Channels to Diverse 50+ Communities**
- Ethnic media are the primary/trusted news sources for immigrant and ethnic communities
- 50+ populations in Asian, Hispanic, Black, and other communities rely on ethnic media for health, financial, and community information
- Language accessibility: Many 50+ community members prefer content in heritage languages

**2. Health Equity & Disparities**
- AARP has stated commitment to disrupting racial and ethnic health disparities
- Ethnic media are essential channels for communicating about:
  - Medicare/Medicaid enrollment
  - Prescription drug costs (Stop Rx Greed campaign)
  - Fraud prevention (especially important for immigrant communities)
  - Caregiving resources
  - Chronic disease management
  - Social Security and retirement planning

**3. Community Trust & Credibility**
- Ethnic media outlets have multi-generational relationships in their communities
- Editorial partnerships with ethnic media demonstrate AARP's authentic commitment to diversity
- Sponsoring ECM event shows support for small, community-based media enterprises

**4. Amplification of AARP Messages**
- Event attendees are editors, publishers, reporters from NJ ethnic media
- Relationships built = ongoing coverage opportunities for AARP initiatives
- Stories about AARP programs reaching diverse communities through trusted media channels

**MEDIA PARTNERSHIPS:**

**COMMUNITY GOODWILL:**

**MISSION ALIGNMENT:**

## Counter-Arguments & Responses

### "We already sponsor national journalist associations"
**RESPONSE:**
- This is a local/state-level complement to national partnerships
- NJ-based ethnic media reach specific communities you want to engage in the state
- Smaller investment, direct local impact
- State office can build relationships with outlets in their territory

### "Limited budget for events"
**RESPONSE:**
- Consider in-kind sponsorship (materials, resources, AARP Magazine subscriptions as raffle prizes)
- Smaller sponsorship tier still provides visibility and networking
- Compared to advertising in ethnic media, sponsorship includes relationship-building
- Part of outreach budget rather than advertising budget

### "How do we measure ROI?"
**RESPONSE:**
- Track media placements mentioning AARP in ethnic outlets (pre- and post-event)
- Count new ethnic media relationships/contacts added to database
- Social media impressions from event coverage
- Attendance of AARP staff at ethnic media events
- Long-term: Membership growth in diverse communities

### "We need to check with national/legal/brand team"
**RESPONSE:**
- Offer to provide additional materials for review
- Note that event celebrates ethnic media (non-political, non-controversial)
- Happy to connect with national multicultural engagement team
- Can structure sponsorship to meet brand guidelines
