## The Wawa Foundation: Eligibility Requirements (DETAILED)

### Basic Eligibility Criteria
1. **Legal Status:** Must be a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization
2. **Geographic Location:** Must operate in Wawa's six-state market area (PA, NJ, DE, MD, VA, FL, DC)
3. **Focus Area Alignment:** **MUST fall into one of three focus areas** (see below)

### Three Mandatory Focus Areas

#### 1. Health
- **What They Fund:**
  - Life-saving research and care for people in need
  - Grants to hospitals with focus on pediatric institutions
  - Wellness programs
  - Example: $200,000 breast cancer support fund

#### 2. Hunger
- **What They Fund:**
  - Food insecurity initiatives
  - Support for Feeding America food bank partners (PRIORITY)
  - Hunger relief programs
  - Food donations to local pantries through Wawa Share
  - Examples:
    - 30+ year partnership with Community FoodBank of New Jersey (CFBNJ)
    - $100,000 donation to Food Bank of South Jersey for Summer Meals program

#### 3. Everyday Heroes
- **What They Fund:**
  - Support for military, veterans, first responders
  - Education and mentoring of at-risk youth (grades K-12)
  - Crisis response
  - Blood drives
  - Volunteer programs
  - Examples: Special Olympics New Jersey, American Red Cross

### Explicit Exclusions (What They DO NOT Fund)

**The Wawa Foundation does NOT provide grants to:**
1. Organizations not exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code
2. Religious organizations (unless the program benefits a large portion of community without regard to religious affiliation)
3. Political causes, candidates, organizations, or campaigns
4. Organizations whose primary purpose is to influence legislation
5. **Arts programs** (explicitly stated)
6. **Sports programs** (explicitly stated)

**CRITICAL NOTE:** No mention of media, journalism, communications, or diversity/equity initiatives in their funding priorities.

## Grant Programs & Application Process

### Foundation Grants (Over $2,500)
- **Amount:** $2,500 and above
- **Submission Windows:** January, April, July, October only
- **Next Window:** January 2026 (CURRENT) or April 2026
- **Process:** Submit grant request and Letter of Inquiry during submission window
- **Timeline:** Organizations receive response before next grant submission window opens
- **Status for Feb 26 Event:** TIMELINE VERY TIGHT - January window would need immediate action

### Local Connection Grants (Under $2,500)
- **Amount:** Less than $2,500
- **Submission:** Rolling basis while budgeted funds last
- **Timeline:** 4-week response time after online inquiry submission
- **Budget Limitation:** Once annual budget is reached, further inquiries declined until following year
- **Sponsorship Range:** Typical sponsorships $250-$2,500

### In-Kind Support: Wawa Community Care
- **What's Included:**
  - Soft pretzels
  - Wawa branded water
  - Gift baskets
  - Free hot beverage coupons
- **Requirements:**
  - At least 3 weeks advance notice
  - Products must be picked up at local store
  - Products cannot be resold
- **Process:** Contact local Wawa store

## Why ECM Does NOT Qualify for Wawa Foundation

### Primary Disqualification Factors

1. **Focus Area Misalignment (FATAL FLAW)**
   - ECM mission centers on ethnic and community media/journalism
   - This does NOT align with Health, Hunger, or Everyday Heroes
   - No flexibility indicated in focus area requirements - "Organizations must fall into The Wawa Foundation's three key areas of focus"

2. **Arts/Communications Exclusion**
   - Wawa Foundation explicitly does not fund arts programs
   - Media/journalism could be classified as communications/arts
   - No evidence of any media or journalism organization receiving Wawa Foundation grants

3. **No Diversity/Media Initiatives**
   - Research found no connection between Wawa Foundation and:
     - Ethnic media organizations
     - Community journalism
     - Media diversity initiatives
     - Communications or journalism programs
   - Wawa Inc. (corporate) has diversity & inclusion commitments, but these are separate from Foundation giving priorities

4. **Event vs. Program Funding**
   - Foundation appears to prioritize ongoing programs over one-time events
   - Examples show support for organizational missions rather than individual events
   - Exception: CFBNJ's events (24-Hour Pack-A-Thon, Blue Jean Ball) but these align with Hunger focus area

2. Explicit exclusion of arts programs suggests media/communications also excluded
3. No evidence of any media or journalism organization receiving Wawa Foundation funding
4. Time investment in application unlikely to yield results given fundamental misalignment

**Documented for Future Reference:**

## Conclusion

The user's initial assessment that ECM "doesn't look like we qualify" for Wawa Foundation support is **100% accurate**. The Foundation's rigid focus on Health, Hunger, and Everyday Heroes, combined with explicit exclusion of arts programs and no history of media/journalism funding, makes this a clear non-fit.

**Strategic Recommendation:** Document this research, skip Wawa Foundation entirely, and invest limited time/resources in more promising sponsorship prospects that explicitly support journalism, media, diversity, or ethnic communities. If $250-$2,500 would be meaningful, make a quick email inquiry to LocalMarketing@wawa.com with a January 17 decision deadline, but don't invest significant time in pursuit.
